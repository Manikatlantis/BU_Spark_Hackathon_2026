04 · THE MATHEMATICS

LEAST-SQUARES.
PLANE FITTING.
ADA SCORING.

Every clipped ROI is reduced to three coefficients using numpy.linalg.lstsq(). From those coefficients, two physical quantities are extracted and compared against federal thresholds.

STEP 1 · LEAST-SQUARES PLANE FIT
z = ax + by + c
Design matrix A = [x, y, 1] stacked for every point in the ROI. Solve Ax = b via least-squares to get the best-fit surface plane through all LiDAR returns. Coefficients a, b encode the slope; c is the intercept.
STEP 2 · SURFACE NORMAL VECTOR
n = [−a, −b, 1]
The plane's normal vector. A perfectly level sidewalk gives n = [0,0,1]. Any deviation in a or b means the surface tilts — and the magnitude of that tilt is cross-slope.
STEP 3 · CROSS-SLOPE (ADA CRITICAL)
α = |a| ÷ √(1+a²+b²)
Perpendicular tilt of the surface, normalized by |n|. Multiply ×100 for %. ADA §4.3.7 max = 2%. We found values up to 22.24% — over 11× the legal limit.
0%ADA LIMIT 2% → DETECTED 22.24%
STEP 4 · VERTICAL STEP DETECTION
Δz = max(zi+1 − zi)
Project points onto travel axis, sort by position, detect z-discontinuities. Jump > 6mm → crack or raised panel. ADA §4.5.2 max vertical obstacle = 6mm.
