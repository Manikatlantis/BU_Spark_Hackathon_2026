01 · THE INVISIBLE CRISIS

61 MILLION AMERICANS.
INVISIBLE TO
CITY PLANNERS.

61 million adults in the United States live with a disability. For wheelchair users, people with visual impairments, and those using mobility aids, a single cracked or tilted sidewalk panel is not an inconvenience — it is a wall.

The Americans with Disabilities Act has mandated a maximum cross-slope of 2% on pedestrian pathways since 1990. Over three decades later, compliance is almost never measured at scale. Cities rely on manual reports, 311 calls, and occasional visual surveys. The math is simply never done.

34
years since the ADA was signed into law.
Sidewalk cross-slope compliance still has no automated, scalable measurement standard in U.S. cities.
02 · WHO GETS LEFT BEHIND

THE MOST
VULNERABLE.
THE LEAST HEARD.

This is not an abstract policy problem. A tilted sidewalk panel has radically different consequences depending on who you are.

♿
WHEELCHAIR USERS
A cross-slope above 2% causes a manual wheelchair to veer uncontrollably into traffic. Power chairs tip. The user cannot safely travel in a straight line. In Brookline, we detected slopes of 22.24% — over 11× the legal maximum.
🦯
PEOPLE WITH VISUAL IMPAIRMENTS
Vertical steps above 6mm (ADA §4.5.2) are undetectable by white cane until contact is made — causing falls. We found steps as small as 6mm and as large as 13mm+ on Brookline sidewalks.
🏠
UNHOUSED PEOPLE & THE ELDERLY
People experiencing homelessness rely on sidewalks as their primary — often only — infrastructure. Elderly residents with walkers or canes face compounded risk from cracked panels. These populations have no political mechanism to report hazards. Their safety is invisible to city dashboards.

06 · THE MATHEMATICS

LEAST-SQUARES.
PLANE FITTING.
ADA SCORING.

Every clipped ROI is reduced to three coefficients. From those, two physical quantities are extracted and compared against federal thresholds.

STEP 1 · LEAST-SQUARES PLANE FIT
z = ax + by + c
Design matrix A = [x, y, 1] stacked for every LiDAR point in the ROI. Solve Ax = b via numpy.linalg.lstsq. Coefficients a, b encode the slope; c is the elevation intercept.
STEP 2 · SURFACE NORMAL VECTOR
n = [−a, −b, 1]
A perfectly level sidewalk gives n = [0, 0, 1]. Any tilt shifts a or b from zero — the magnitude of that deviation is cross-slope.
STEP 3 · CROSS-SLOPE — ADA CRITICAL
α = |a| ÷ √(1+a²+b²)
Perpendicular tilt normalized by the plane's normal magnitude. ×100 = %. ADA §4.3.7 max: 2%. We found 22.24% — over 11× the legal limit.
0%ADA LIMIT 2% → MAX DETECTED 22.24%
STEP 4 · VERTICAL STEP DETECTION
Δz = max(zi+1−zi)
Sort points by travel axis, detect z-discontinuities. Jump > 6mm → raised panel or crack. ADA §4.5.2 maximum vertical obstacle: 6mm.
